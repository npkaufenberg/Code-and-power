Tech Hero: Marian Rogers

Marian Rogers Croak is a Vice President of Engineering at Google. Previously, she held the position of Senior Vice President of Research and Development at AT&T. With over 200 patents to her name, she has made significant contributions to technology. In 2013, she was inducted into the Women in Technology International Hall of Fame. Recently, in 2022, Croak received another prestigious honor: induction into the National Inventors Hall of Fame for her groundbreaking patent related to VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol) Technology. Her invention revolutionized communication by allowing users to make calls over the internet instead of traditional phone lines. Today, VoIP technology plays a crucial role in remote work and conferencing.
